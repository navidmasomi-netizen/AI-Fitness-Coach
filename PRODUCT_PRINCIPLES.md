# PRODUCT PRINCIPLES — AI-Fitness-Coach

## 1. Product Purpose

AI-Fitness-Coach exists to help users train better, eat better, recover better and stay consistent.

The product should behave like a coach, not like a spreadsheet.

---

## 2. Target User

The target user is someone who wants guidance.

They may not know:

- what to train today
- how many sets to do
- when to increase weight
- what to eat before training
- how to recover
- how to stay consistent

The product should reduce uncertainty.

---

## 3. Core Product Principle

Users should spend more time training than using the app.

The app must be fast, clear and practical under real gym conditions.

---

## 4. Guidance Over Tracking

Tracking is not enough.

Every tracked action should eventually help the user make a better decision.

Examples:

- set logs should feed progression
- workout history should show improvement
- profile data should shape the program
- nutrition data should guide meal timing

---

## 5. Reduce Cognitive Load

Every screen should reduce thinking.

The user should always know:

- what to do now
- what comes next
- why it matters

If a screen creates confusion, it is not finished.

---

## 6. No Fake Intelligence

Do not fake AI.

Do not create artificial suggestions that are not supported by data.

Recommendations must be:

- deterministic
- explainable
- based on user data
- safe

AI can be added later, but the foundation must remain reliable.

---

## 7. Backend-Driven Coaching

Coaching logic should live in the backend.

The mobile app should display guidance, not invent it.

This keeps the product scalable and consistent across platforms.

---

## 8. Minimal First, Smart Later

Build the simplest useful version first.

Avoid:

- premature AI
- excessive dashboards
- complex analytics
- unnecessary gamification

Add intelligence only when the core flow is stable.

---

## 9. Gym-Ready UX

The app must be usable when the user is:

- tired
- sweaty
- distracted
- holding the phone with one hand
- in the middle of a workout

Buttons must be clear.
Inputs must be fast.
Errors must be understandable.

---

## 10. Personalization Must Be Earned

Do not ask users too many questions too early.

First, help them start.

Then collect profile data progressively.

Full personalization should come after basic clarity.

---

## 11. Motivation Without Noise

Motivation should be subtle.

Avoid fake hype.

Prefer useful feedback such as:

- next session cue
- progression insight
- previous session comparison
- consistency reminders

---

## 12. Safety First

Fitness recommendations must be conservative by default.

Progression should not be aggressive.

Injury flags, fatigue and recovery should eventually influence recommendations.

---

## 13. What We Should Not Build Too Early

Do not build too early:

- full nutrition engine
- full AI coach
- push notifications
- heavy charts
- social feed
- complex gamification
- marketplace
- trainer marketplace

These can come later after the workout core is validated.

---

## 14. Product North Star

Every day the app should answer:

> What should I do today to improve my body?

If a feature does not help answer this question, it is probably not a priority.
